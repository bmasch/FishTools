Introduction
To help you prepare to understand your employer's written policy, this course includes the minimum requirements for all written policies. Click each heading below to review these requirements.

Remember that your employer will ask you to sign an acknowledgement of receipt form when you receive your company's written policy.

Address All Elements
Address the use of alcohol, prescription medications, over-the-counter medications or illegal drugs, including (a) which drug or alcohol tests will be used, (b) the cutoff levels for each, (c) what testing procedures and protocols will be followed, and (d) a clear statement that supervisors will be trained regarding their responsibilities related to various testing prior to the implementation of any testing associated with this program.
Articulate All Elements
Articulate all the elements of the level of the Drug Free Safety Program which the employer is implementing.
Clarify Employment Issues
Clarify that nothing in the policy alters the employment-at-will status (if applicable) or affects any other employment issues.
Communicate Policy
Communicate the drug-free program and policy through an initial presentation to all employees prior to implementing the program and annually through employee education sessions.
Comply With Regulations
Comply with all pertinent federal and state laws or regulations.
Describe Referrals
Describe how referrals may be made for testing, assessment, and employee assistance.
Identify an Administrator
Identify a DFSP administrator and explain the person's role or responsibilities with regard to the drug-free program.
Include Confidentiality Talks
Include a discussion of confidentiality of the the program to insure the privacy rights of individuals.
Reference Available Help
Reference the help available to employees with substance abuse problems.
State Important Issues
Each written policy must state:
  • management’s reason for creating a substance-free workplace, namely its concerns for employee safety and health, productivity, accident prevention and loss control, and a commitment to rehabilitation.
  • clearly that the program applies to all employees, including all levels of management.
  • that law enforcement authorities may be contacted and requested to come onto the employer’s property, when appropriate, in conjunction with a referral for criminal prosecution.
  • what is prohibited and the consequences of a violation of this policy for employees.
  • the consequences, if any, for an employee’s refusal to submit to a medical examination or a drug or alcohol test in conjunction with the operation of the employer’s DFWP Program.
  • the consequences for any employee attempting to manipulate the drug/alcohol testing process, such as trying to adulterate a specimen.
  • that an employee’s violation under the DFSP policy shall not be reported to law enforcement official unless required by a regulatory body or by criminal law provisions.
